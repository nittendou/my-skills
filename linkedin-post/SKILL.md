# LinkedIn Post Skill

Writes LinkedIn posts in CY's authentic voice: grounded in practice, oriented toward teaching,
and written to spark conversation — not to broadcast or promote.

---

## Voice Profile

- **Practitioner-first**: Leads with what happened or what was built
- **Educator lens**: Connects to what it means for students or the next generation
- **Systems thinker**: Sees cause and effect, upstream/downstream implications
- **Accessible**: Avoids jargon; if a term is used, it's briefly explained
- **Curious, not prescriptive**: Asks questions; doesn't lecture

---

## Post Types

| Type | When to use | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| **Reaction** | News event, industry development | Observational, questioning |
| **Build** | Tool, skill, repo, deck created | Practitioner pride |
| **Teach** | Course, lesson, case study | Educator, accessible |
| **Insight** | Pattern noticed, lesson learned | Reflective, genuine |

---

## How to Use This Skill

### Step 1 — Understand the source material
May come from: a news item, finished artifact, teaching moment, or topic.
If from news-to-classroom, use that teaching note as the foundation.

### Step 2 — Write 2 variants
- **Variant A**: Practitioner / SCM professional audience
- **Variant B**: Educator / student audience

### Step 3 — Structure each post

**Hook** (1–2 lines): Concrete fact or direct statement. No "I'm excited to share..."
**Body** (3–6 lines): Context, insight, implication. Short paragraphs.
**CTA** (1 line): Open question or clear call to action.
**Hashtags** (4–6): Always include domain tag + #Education or #Teaching if relevant.

---

## Format Rules

- Length: 150–250 words per variant
- No em-dashes in body — use colons or line breaks instead
- Never start with "I'm excited to…" or "Just launched…"
- Emojis: 0–2 per post, only if they add clarity
- Always include [link] placeholder where a URL belongs

---

## Output Format

```
VARIANT A — Practitioner angle
[Post text]
#Hashtags

VARIANT B — Educator angle
[Post text]
#Hashtags

→ Replace [link] with your URL before posting.
```
